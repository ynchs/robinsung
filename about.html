About

I'm a partner marketing lead based in San Francisco, specializing in building bridges between technology companies and enterprise buyers across borders.

Background

I approach partner marketing with a perspective informed by behavioral research and systems thinking. My background — sociology at NYU, UX design, and now growth marketing — taught me to design experiences that guide behavior, not just communicate value.

As a Korean-American, I naturally bridge the gap between Asian tech companies and U.S. enterprise buyers. I've spent the past three years translating technical innovation into American business language, and complex procurement processes into actionable partner guidance.

For the past three years, I've led Korea's national tech pavilion at major U.S. conferences — TechCrunch Disrupt, RSA Conference, and SEMICON West — helping 90+ companies generate measurable pipeline with Fortune 500 enterprises including Google, Tesla, Meta, Intel, and Amazon.

Philosophy

I don't follow existing playbooks — I build systems that partners can scale independently while maintaining quality standards. When I didn't have a CRM budget, I built tracking infrastructure in Google Sheets. When partners needed enablement, I created the curriculum myself. When booth designs didn't meet standards, I redesigned them.

Most importantly: I care if my partners succeed. If they spend months preparing for an event, fly to San Francisco, invest $50K in a booth, then get zero results because the follow-up system was broken — that's on me.
